## Summary

**93 findings** across eight organizational angles. The most severe pattern is file size: 37 `.rs` files exceed 500 lines, with 8 files over 700 lines and 2 files over 1,400 lines. The second most severe pattern is crate-graph layering violations (`vyre-driver-wgpu` importing from both `vyre-runtime` and the `vyre` meta-crate) and broken path dependencies (`surgec-grammar-gen`). Naming inconsistency (`run`/`dispatch`/`execute`/`invoke`, `Program`/`IR`/`Bytecode`, `backend`/`driver`/`adapter`) makes the codebase harder to learn than it needs to be. README coverage is thin or absent for more than half the crates. Docstring quality is good in `vyre-driver` and `vyre-foundation` but collapses in `surgec` due to a crate-level `allow(missing_errors_doc)`.

**Quality score: 4/10.** The vyre foundation crates show discipline; surgec and the backend integration layers do not. Torvalds-standard organization is not yet achieved.
